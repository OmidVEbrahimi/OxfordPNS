Course Details

Overview

The Oxford Psychological Networks School offers an intensive, comprehensive program focused on the theory and application of network analysis in psychological research. This workshop is designed for researchers, graduate students, and professionals who want to expand their methodological toolkit with cutting-edge network analysis techniques.

Course Topics

  • Introduction to Network Theory: Fundamental concepts and applications in psychology
  • Network Estimation: Methods for estimating psychological networks from data
  • Network Visualization: Creating effective and informative network graphics
  • Network Comparison: Techniques for comparing networks across groups and time
  • Dynamic Networks: Modeling temporal dynamics in psychological systems
  • Software Tools: Hands-on training with R packages (qgraph, bootnet, NetworkComparisonTest)

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of network analysis in psychology
  • Estimate and visualize psychological networks using state-of-the-art methods
  • Interpret network structures and identify key nodes and connections
  • Compare networks across different groups or time points
  • Apply network analysis to their own research questions
  • Critically evaluate network studies in the literature

Prerequisites

Participants should have basic knowledge of statistics and some experience with R programming. Familiarity with regression analysis and multivariate statistics is helpful but not required.

Schedule

Duration: 5 days intensive workshop

Format: Lectures, hands-on practical sessions, and group discussions

Daily Schedule:

  • Morning sessions: 9:00 AM - 12:30 PM
  • Lunch break: 12:30 PM - 2:00 PM
  • Afternoon sessions: 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Materials

All course materials, including lecture slides, R scripts, and datasets, will be provided to participants. Participants should bring their own laptops with R and RStudio installed.
