Oxford Psychological Networks School (OxPNS)
22 to 26 June, 2026 | 5-Day Course | Oxford, UK
Overview

The Oxford Psychological Networks School (OxPNS) is a comprehensive training programme focused on contemporary network analytic approaches in psychological, social and medical sciences. The course integrates conceptual foundations with applied modelling techniques, enabling participants to work confidently with statistical network models in their own research.

What You Will Learn
  • The theoretical foundations of the network perspective
  • An understanding of how the network perspective links to network science and complex systems
  • Guidance on formulating research questions suitable for different network analytic applications
  • Practical skills for preparing and analysing newly collected and secondary data
  • Training in a broad range of cross-sectional network models, including for:
    • Binary data
    • Ordinal and continuous data
  • Advanced dynamic network models for longitudinal data, including:
    • Panel or cohort data
    • Experience Sampling Method (ESM) or Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA) data
    • Idiographic (N=1) analyses
  • Expertise in estimating, interpreting and writing reports based on network analytic methods for:
    • Studying risk, resilience, and protective factors through a systems-based perspective
    • Onset and maintenance mechanisms of different phenomena
    • Group comparisons (e.g. gender, age, subgroup contrasting)
    • Assessment of the co-occurrence of two phenomena (e.g. comorbidity)
    • Granular intervention effects
    • Vicious cycles and feedback loops
Who Should Attend
  • Research staff and students
  • Open to participants from Oxford and internationally
  • The course is suitable for individuals in psychology, psychiatry, medicine, and the social sciences interested in learning about statistical network analysis.
